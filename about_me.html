I have been working with computers since I was 15 years old. What started as a gaming hobby transitioned
into much more when my computer started malfunctioning and I couldnt play my favourite game.
I immediately picked up the phone and called tech support, but was given the run around,
and never got help with my issue. Luckily, I could still connect to the internet, and frustrated,
I started looking for a solution to my problem.

After several days of searching, and trial and error I fixed my computer. I was very proud
and this encouraged me, and got me very interested in the field of information technology. I bought books,
I read a lot, and I found the right people on the internet who answered my questions when I ran into trouble.

It was a very rocky road. I was often so frustrated that I wanted to just drop everyhting and never
come back to it again, but I dont get discouraged easily, and my meticulous, methodic nature paid off.
After destroying several expensive parts which I spent months saving for, I really learned my lesson and from that point
I was very successful with my computer builds.

Eventually, I became confident enough to get certified as a professional computer technician. I studied
for a few months and then I went and passed the Comp TIA A+ exam on my 1st try.

Right now, I am more experienced than ever, thanks to my job in a large educational organization where
I run into various problems and have to develop solutions on the fly. I am certain of my abilities
and there is no probem I canot solve as long as a solution exists.

Right now, I am gradurating with a Bachelors degree in Data Communications, and I plan to propel my career to new heights with a MCTS and Cisco certifications which I am studying for.
